Projects / work

RMI Intership

June – July 2025

During my internship at the Royal Meteorological Institute (RMI) in Brussels, I worked on a project focused on the calibration and monitoring of precipitation radars. The main objective was to develop a software tool that automates the calibration process, thereby ensuring accurate and reliable radar measurements.
Producing precise radar measurements is a key requirement for improving downstream meteorological products, such as quantitative precipitation estimates and weather forecasting models. My role involved designing and implementing data processing algorithms, developing a user-friendly interface, and preparing the integration of the tool into RMI’s existing operational systems. While full integration could not be completed within the timeframe of the internship, it is planned for my return to the institute, where I will continue and finalize this work.
In addition to the main project, I developed an auxiliary radar image visualization tool to support analysis and facilitate daily work, improving both efficiency and data interpretation. This internship allowed me to apply my technical skills in a real-world operational environment, while deepening my understanding of meteorological instrumentation and data analysis.

Météo-France intership

April – July 2024

I also completed an internship at Météo-France, where I developed a tool for the intercomparison of two precipitation radars. The objective was to assess the consistency and performance of radar measurements by analyzing and comparing their outputs.
The tool was implemented as an automated script, without a graphical user interface, and was designed to be run manually for specific case studies. Despite its initial standalone usage, the tool is currently being integrated into the operational radar monitoring chain, highlighting its relevance and reliability for long-term use.
As part of this project, I designed and improved a BUFR binary file decoder, rebuilding it from scratch to ensure robustness, flexibility, and better adaptability to the radar data formats in use. This work significantly strengthened my skills in low-level data handling, binary formats, and data processing pipelines, while deepening my understanding of meteorological observation systems.

International

As part of my engineering degree, I am required to complete 17 weeks abroad, an experience that I view as a key opportunity for both professional and personal development. In addition to this academic requirement, I have already had the chance to take part in several international trips.

During the summer of 2025, I completed an internship at the Royal Meteorological Institute in Brussels, where I worked on the calibration and monitoring of precipitation radars. I was part of a small, diverse team of about fifteen people from various countries, including Germany, Ukraine, Peru, and the Netherlands.
